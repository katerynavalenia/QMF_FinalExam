PART A — ARGUMENT: GENUINE MONETARY SOVEREIGNTY AND THE TREATMENT EFFECT

Ukraine exercised genuine monetary sovereignty during three distinct episodes:
(1) the brief devaluation periods of 2008–09 and 2014–15, when the exchange rate
served as a critical shock absorber, and (2) the inflation-targeting period of
2016–2021, when the NBU operated as an independent central bank with a modern
policy framework. During the remaining years — the dollar-peg periods of 2000–2008
and 2009–2014, and the wartime peg from February 2022 — Ukraine had no effective
monetary sovereignty; the exchange rate anchor constrained monetary policy through
the impossible trinity, much as Euro Area membership would have.

This asymmetry has a direct implication for the counterfactual analysis in Part B.
The "treatment" of Euro Area membership is not constant over time. During peg
periods, Ukraine was already operating under a fixed exchange rate regime (anchored
to the US dollar rather than the euro). Switching from a USD peg to the euro would
have changed the anchor currency but not fundamentally altered the monetary regime.
The treatment effect of EA membership is therefore concentrated in the devaluation
episodes — where the exchange-rate buffer would have been unavailable — and in the
2016–2021 inflation-targeting period — where an independent NBU would have been
replaced by the ECB. The counterfactual must reflect this time-varying treatment
intensity: the gap between actual and counterfactual inflation should be largest
during episodes of genuine monetary autonomy.

Furthermore, the credibility channel (Giavazzi & Pagano, 1988; Barro & Gordon, 1983)
suggests that the inflation differential should be larger in the pre-2016 period,
when the NBU lacked a credible nominal anchor, and smaller after 2016, when inflation
targeting partially imported the discipline that EA membership would have provided.

Sources: IMF AREAER (2000–2024); NBU Annual Reports; Calvo & Reinhart (2002);
Åslund (2015); Gorodnichenko (2014) "Ukraine's Exchange Rate Policy".
